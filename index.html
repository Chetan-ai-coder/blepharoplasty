THE PROCEDURE

What Is
Blepharoplasty?

Blepharoplasty is a surgical procedure that corrects excess skin, herniated fat and laxity of the upper and/or lower eyelids — rejuvenating the eye area while always preserving your natural anatomy.

Beyond the aesthetic improvement, when upper eyelid skin obstructs the visual field, the procedure can also restore peripheral vision — making it a functional as well as cosmetic treatment.

Every plan is individually designed around your facial proportions, skin quality and eyelid function, and performed personally by Drª. Forsati under local anesthesia with light sedation.

Surgical Techniques

Upper Eyelid Blepharoplasty
Removes excess skin and fat from the upper eyelid, lifting hooding and restoring an open, refreshed look. The incision is hidden within the natural eyelid crease.
Lower Eyelid Blepharoplasty
Treats under-eye bags and skin laxity, smoothing the lower lid contour for a rested, even appearance.
Combined Upper & Lower Blepharoplasty
Treats both eyelids in a single surgical session for complete, balanced eye rejuvenation.
Transconjunctival Fat Repositioning
A scarless inner-eyelid approach that repositions herniated fat rather than removing it — ideal for under-eye bags without excess skin.
Canthopexy (Eyelid Support)
Reinforces the outer corner of the eye to prevent lower lid sagging and maintain a youthful eye shape — often combined with lower blepharoplasty.
Functional Blepharoplasty
Removes upper eyelid skin that obstructs the visual field — improving both vision and appearance at once.

Combination Procedures

Ptosis Correction
Addresses drooping of the upper eyelid caused by a weakened muscle, often performed alongside blepharoplasty for complete eyelid rejuvenation.
Combined With Brow Lift
Pairs naturally with a brow or forehead lift when upper hooding originates from brow descent rather than eyelid skin alone.

Common Questions

Frequently
Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your surgical consultation.

Does blepharoplasty hurt? +
The procedure is performed under local anesthesia with light sedation, so discomfort during surgery is minimal. Mild tightness or soreness is normal during the first few days of recovery.
How long is the recovery? +
Most patients return to non-strenuous daily activities within a week, once sutures are removed. Exercise and contact lenses typically resume after 2–3 weeks.
Will there be visible scarring? +
Incisions are placed within the eyelid's natural crease, or inside the lower lid for the transconjunctival approach — making scars virtually invisible once healed.
When will I see final results? +
Bruising and swelling subside gradually over 1–2 weeks, with final results visible at 4–6 weeks as residual swelling resolves.
Can it be combined with other treatments? +
Yes. Blepharoplasty is frequently paired with a brow lift, ptosis correction, or non-surgical treatments such as Botulinum Toxin for complete facial rejuvenation.
